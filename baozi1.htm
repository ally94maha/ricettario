Pork baozi

Ingredients

  • 300g All-purpose flour
  • 1/2 tsp Quick dry yeast
  • 1/2 tsp Sugar
  • 1/4 tsp Baking powder (optional)
  • 200ml 40C warm water

  • 200g Pork belly
  • 100g Ground pork
  • 6 canned water chestnuts
  • 3 spring onions
  • 2 hot peppers
  • 1/2 tsp Salt
  • 1/2 tsp Soy sauce
  • 1/2 tsp White pepper
  • 1 tsp Sesame oil
  • 1/2 tsp Fish sauce

Method

  1. Mix flour, yeast, sugar and oil in a bowl. Pour in water little by little while mixing.
  2. Combine and knead with your hands until a very smooth dough forms.
  3. Place the dough in a well-oiled bowl and leave it in a warm place until it doubles in size, approximately 2 hours.
  4. Prepare the filling
  6. Knead the dough for 2 minutes to get rid of any air pockets. Weigh and divide the dough into equal pieces (each should weigh about 48g-50g).
  7. Take each dough ball, and with a rolling pin, roll it from the edge towards the center, without actually rolling the center of the dough. The goal is to flatten the dough into a round circle with a thinner edge and thicker center.
  8. Place some filling in the middle of a wrapper then fold into a bao shape.
  9. The buns are folded with one hand holding the skin and filling, and the other hand pleating the edges of the dough disk like an accordion.
  10. Leave all the assembled buns to rest for another 15 minutes or so.
  11. Then you can steam or pan-fry them.
